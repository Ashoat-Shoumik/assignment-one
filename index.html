Tesla features

Autopilot

Autopilot Available since around 2015, Autopilot started as an extra feature you could add for a hefty fee to complement your driving. The advanced driving system would auto-steer, keep up with other vehicles, brake for slower traffic, and generally help out with highway driving, as long as the driver kept eyes on the road and hands on the wheel.

Supercharging

Supercharging Tesla has its own charging network, exclusive to Tesla owners. Other EVs (like Rivian and Jeep) are starting to build out their own networks as well, but none are as extensive as Tesla's. Supercharging is available across the U.S. and in other countries where Teslas are sold: There are more than 25,000 superchargers. The fast charging adds up to 200 miles.

Dog mode

Dog mode Just like it sounds like, Dog mode is for dogs to chill in the car. Parked cars overheat and are unsafe for dogs, but with this mode engaged, the car is kept cool with the air system still on even with the car in park. Plus, an on-screen message on the center console says "My owner will be back soon" to let passersby know your dog is safe.
